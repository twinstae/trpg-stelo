Magic Items can have near-limitless forms and features, entire
books have been dedicated to different ones. While more Magic
Items will be added to the final version of Dungeon World 2, here
are some tools to easily create your own.

When creating magic item, choose its Form, its Origin, and either
one Effect, or one Cost and two Effects, then create any moves or
similar effects it might require.

Magic Item Categories
Create a Simple Effect
These are usually minor trinkets often used to add descriptive
flair, but can very powerful if used creatively. They don’t have
a rolled or complex Move associated with them, but can have a
straightforward Move. Examples include: Sending Stone, Bag of
Holding, Tricksy Rope, Tears of Analise

Augment an Existing Move
These items take a Move or asset the user already has access to
and alters it some way, usually empowering it. Examples include:
Lamented Momento, Cloak of Absurd Protection.

Bestow a New Move
These grant their user access to a whole new Move - one that may
or may not be related to their existing Moves. Examples include:
Farsight Stone, Flying Carpet, Wax Wings.

Bestow Multiple Moves
These items are typically especially powerful and complex, and
their Moves are often directly related or lead into each other.
Examples include: Sphere of Annihilation.

Limited Use
Some items may be destroyed by use, have a limited charge, or
have a custom Move that destroys them as part of a 6- or 7-9 roll
on a custom Move. Examples include: Ambrosia, Spell Scroll, Oil of
Etherelness, Potion, Magic Wand.

Cursed Items
Curses are negative effects that can sometimes come with a
magic item, and often can’t easily be removed once manifested.
When creating a cursed item, make sure the curse maintains
their agency, creating difficult consequences and choices, not
removing their agency entirely.
